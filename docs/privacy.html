Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Muse is designed around a single principle: your conversations are yours alone. This policy describes what data Muse does and does not collect.

What Muse does not collect

  • No account registration or sign-in
  • No conversation content transmitted off your device
  • No usage analytics or behavioural tracking
  • No crash reports sent to any server
  • No advertising identifiers

Muse may ask for your first name in Settings to personalise the greeting on the home screen. This is stored only in your device's local app preferences and is never transmitted.

On-device AI inference

All AI responses are generated entirely on your iPhone using a locally bundled language model (Llama 3.2 1B). Your prompts and the model's responses never leave your device.

Chat history

Your conversations are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework so the assistant can remember context across turns. This data is never transmitted anywhere. You can clear all conversations at any time from the trash icon on the chat screen, the History tab, or by deleting the app.

Topic graph (Aura)

Muse classifies each of your prompts into one of seven on-device topic categories (AI, Code, Data, Personal, Health, Creative, Work) using Apple's on-device NLEmbedding framework. The category labels and visit counts are stored locally to power the Aura graph. Nothing about this classification leaves your device.

Sharing to other apps

When you tap the share icon on a response, iOS's standard share sheet is presented so you can copy the text into WhatsApp, Messages, Notes, or any other app you choose. Muse only passes the response text to iOS; what the receiving app does with it is governed by that app's own privacy policy.

Microphone

If you use voice input, Muse uses Apple's on-device speech recognition. Audio is transcribed locally and never sent to any server, including Apple's servers — Muse explicitly requires on-device-only recognition.

Network access

Muse requires a network connection only once: on first launch, to download the AI model (~700 MB) from Hugging Face. After that, the app works entirely offline. No conversation data is ever sent over the network.

Third-party services

Muse uses the following open-source libraries, none of which collect personal data:

  • MLX Swift (Apple) — on-device ML inference
  • swift-transformers / swift-huggingface (Hugging Face) — model loading

Children's privacy

Muse does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date.
